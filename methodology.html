The NZVI is comprised of four core factors:

  • Complexity: Measures the diversity of employment in an area. Economically complex areas, with varied industries, are presumed more resilient to economic shifts.
  • Relatedness: Looks at how often industries co-locate, assuming that closely linked industries are less resilient to restructuring if one is threatened.
  • Reliance: Assesses if an area depends heavily on a few industries, especially those at risk from green restructuring (as identified by experts and government publications). Areas with high reliance are considered more vulnerable.
  • Population: Factors in the overall population of an area.

The NZVI calculation uses these indicators to give each Local Authority District a score from 1 to 10. This reflects overall economic resilience and vulnerability, with a score of 10 indicating high vulnerability due to industry reliance and population exposure.

This score allows for a comparative view of risk levels, highlighting areas that may need targeted economic support to better transition to a sustainable economy. A forthcoming journal article will provide a comprehensive explanation of the NZVI methodology, and the authors will share it here upon publication.
