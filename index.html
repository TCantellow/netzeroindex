Net Zero Vulnerability Index (NZVI)

The Net Zero Vulnerability Index (NZVI) offers a comprehensive framework to assess the vulnerability of regions within the UK to socio-economic disruptions arising from the transition to a low-carbon economy. This model addresses the risk of economic restructuring by examining factors such as industry complexity, inter-industry relationships, employment reliance on at-risk sectors, and population size. These components together form a resilience and vulnerability profile that highlights areas most likely to face significant socio-economic impacts as a result of net-zero transitions. In doing so, the NZVI goes beyond simple employment loss metrics, integrating socio-technical elements to reflect both economic and community adaptability to changes in traditional industries.

By assessing employment landscape characteristics (complexity), economic interdependencies (relatedness), dependency on key sectors (reliance), and the proportion of the local population affected, the NZVI provides a multidimensional view of regional vulnerabilities. As sectors vital to the carbon economy transition, some regions are anticipated to experience significant upheavals, such as those heavily reliant on fossil-fuel-based employment. This index sheds light on the broader socio-economic challenges that accompany decarbonization, helping policymakers, local authorities, and researchers identify regions most in need of support in their journey toward a more sustainable economy.
